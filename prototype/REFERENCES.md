# References & Sources

## Neuroscience: Predictive Processing & The Free Energy Principle

1. **Friston, K.** (2010). "The free-energy principle: a unified brain theory?" *Nature Reviews Neuroscience*, 11(2), 127–138. — The foundational paper on predictive processing and precision weighting.

2. **Bastos, A.M., Usrey, W.M., Adams, R.A., Mangun, G.R., Fries, P., & Friston, K.** (2012). "Canonical microcircuits for predictive coding." *Neuron*, 76(4), 695–711. — The cortical circuit implementation of precision-weighted error signaling.

3. **Adams, R.A., Stephan, K.E., Brown, H.R., Frith, C.D., & Friston, K.** (2013). "The computational anatomy of psychosis." *Frontiers in Psychiatry*, 4, 47. — Precision weighting dysregulation as a model for delusion and OCD.

4. **Clark, A.** (2013). "Whatever next? Predictive brains, situated agents, and the future of cognitive science." *Behavioral and Brain Sciences*, 36(3), 181–204. — Accessible overview of predictive processing.

5. **Hohwy, J.** (2013). *The Predictive Mind*. Oxford University Press. — Comprehensive philosophical treatment.

6. **Sterzer, P., Adams, R.A., Fletcher, P.C., Frith, C.D., Lawrie, S.M., Muckli, L., ... & Friston, K.J.** (2018). "The predictive coding account of psychosis." *Neuroscience & Biobehavioral Reviews*, 64, 264–264. — Clinical evidence for precision weighting dysfunction.

## Philosophy of Science: Operationalized Epistemologies

7. **Bridgman, P.W.** (1927). *The Logic of Modern Physics*. Macmillan. — Operationalism: concepts defined entirely by measurement operations.

8. **Mayo, D.G.** (2018). *Statistical Inference as Severe Testing: How to Get Beyond the Statistics Wars*. Cambridge University Press. — Severity as a criterion for warranted inference.

9. **Wimsatt, W.C.** (1981/2007). "Robustness, reliability, and overdetermination." In *Re-Engineering Philosophy for Limited Beings*, Harvard University Press. — Robustness analysis as epistemic criterion.

10. **Pearl, J.** (2000). *Causality: Models, Reasoning, and Inference*. Cambridge University Press. — Do-calculus and structural identifiability.

11. **Sneed, J.D.** (1971). *The Logical Structure of Mathematical Physics*. Reidel. — Structuralist philosophy of science with formal constraints.

12. **Platt, J.R.** (1964). "Strong Inference." *Science*, 146(3642), 347–353. — Systematic elimination of alternative hypotheses.

## AI: Automated Scientific Discovery & Reasoning

13. **Krenn, M., et al.** (2025). "AI-Newton rediscovers fundamental laws of nature from scratch." arXiv:2504.01538. — Autonomous rediscovery of Newton's laws from raw noisy data. The closest existing system to Puzzle Logic.

14. **Baek, J., et al.** (2025). "DeepScientist: Evaluating autonomous AI-driven scientific research." arXiv:2502.13056. — Autonomous hypothesis generation with 21 genuine discoveries from 5,000 hypotheses.

15. **Gerrish, S.M.** (2025). "Cognitive architectures for scientific discovery." arXiv:2504.04130. — Framework for automating the scientific method with LLMs.

16. **Wang, H., et al.** (2023). "Neuro-symbolic AI for scientific discovery." *AI Magazine*. — Neuro-symbolic approaches to scientific reasoning.

## AI: Truth Maintenance & Belief Revision

17. **Doyle, J.** (1979). "A Truth Maintenance System." *Artificial Intelligence*, 12(3), 231–272. — The original TMS for maintaining consistency in reasoning systems.

18. **de Kleer, J.** (1986). "An assumption-based TMS." *Artificial Intelligence*, 28(2), 127–162. — ATMS for efficiently tracking multiple consistent belief sets.

19. **Gardenfors, P.** (1988). *Knowledge in Flux: Modeling the Dynamics of Epistemic States*. MIT Press. — Formal models of belief revision (AGM framework).

## Neural Networks & Skill Acquisition

20. **Hochreiter, S., & Schmidhuber, J.** (1997). "Long short-term memory." *Neural Computation*, 9(8), 1735–1780. — The LSTM architecture as biological synapse analog.

21. **Dreyfus, S.E.** (2004). "The five-stage model of adult skill acquisition." *Bulletin of Science, Technology & Society*, 24(3), 177–181. — Novice → expert skill development, matching the Ω decay curve.

22. **Ericsson, K.A.** (2006). "The influence of experience and deliberate practice on the development of superior expert performance." *Cambridge Handbook of Expertise and Expert Performance*. — Empirical evidence for expertise development through experience.

## Applications: Constraint-Based Systems

23. **Gevorgyan, A., Poolman, M.G., & Fell, D.A.** (2008). "Detection of stoichiometric inconsistencies in biomolecular models." *Bioinformatics*, 24(19), 2245–2251. — COBRA Toolbox and metabolic constraint modeling.

24. **Tamhane, A.C., & Mah, R.S.H.** (1985). "Data reconciliation and gross error detection in chemical process networks." *Technometrics*. — Industrial data reconciliation as puzzle logic.

25. **Fagiano, L., Novara, C., & Milanese, M.** (2020). "Set membership identification." *IEEE Transactions on Automatic Control*. — Parameter falsification via feasible set exclusion.

## Related Concepts

26. **Popper, K.** (1959). *The Logic of Scientific Discovery*. Hutchinson. — Falsificationism as epistemic method.

27. **Kuhn, T.S.** (1962). *The Structure of Scientific Revolutions*. University of Chicago Press. — Paradigm shifts as reassembly of the belief graph.

28. **Chang, H.** (2012). *Is Water H2O? Evidence, Realism and Pluralism*. Springer. — Operational coherence as truth criterion.
